"A delicate balance of nerd, fittness & code."

This story begins with an ill-fitted graphics card and haste to play the Legion expansion of World of Warcraft (which will be referred to as 'WoW' from this point on for everyone's sake). I built my first computer around this ill-fitted graphics card in 2016 and finally made the decision to stop lurking in the shadows of the Charlotte tech community. I began actively participating in Meet-Ups sponsored by Charlotte Devs, Girl Develop It (now Queen City Bytes), and serving as an Associate for the East Coast Game Conference in Raleigh, NC each year. I spent nearly a decade as a cognitive behavioral counselor in the public school system and developed a passion to advocate for higher quality career counseling and early exposure to STEM career paths. Growing up in a low-income area of South Carolina myself while navigating the challenges of being the first member of my family to attend college, I quickly recognized this essential missing component in our public school system today. I know that having more access to STEM education and mentorship would have made a huge impact early on in my life and I want to advocate for providing these resources to our students as early as possible in their educational careers. Before making the decision to leave the public school system to work in tech, I founded a structured educational group entitled #loveyourSELFIE which provides a 10 week group for 5th grade girls to explore STEM careers through a book study and special presentations from local Charlotte women in technology. From this, the "Virtual Field Trip" began at a local public school this summer! I think we've come a long way, but there's even further to go.
